The Enchantments Lottery - March 2025

Background

The Enchantments is a region in Washington state's Cascade Mountain Range that is home to over 700 alpine lakes and ponds surrounded by a vast expanse of mountain peaks. It is one of the most popular outdoor destinations for hikers, campers, rockclimbers, and anyone who enjoys the wilderness. As a result of the popularity, the Washington Trails Association introduced a lottery system to reduce the overcrowding that the region was seeing.

How the Lottery Works

The lottery opens on February 15th and closes On March 1st each year. Each application consists of three entries in order of preference. An individual entry comprises a zone, date, and group size. The preference is determined by order in the application. For example, one application might look like:


Zone Entry Date Group Size
Colchuck 5/15/2024 4
Core Enchantment Zone 8/7/2024 7
Eightmile Zone 7/1/2024 3

An applicant can only apply once, which greatly reduces the odds of an individual winning the lottery. However, winning the lottery is not completely up to chance; there are some factors that can dramatically change your odds of wining the lottery. This project provides some critical insights of how to increase your odds to win the lottery, and it provides some recommendations for how to completely maximize your chances.

Data Overview

There were two sets of data used in this project: the 2024 Enchantment lottery statistics, and the historical weather data for the area. Each observation in the lottery statistics represents an application (like the example above), and it is structured as follows:

  • Lottery Year: the year in which the application was submitted (2024 for all of our data)
  • LotteryID: the ID associated with the application
  • Choice ID: the ID associated with each entry for the application (there will be 3 per 1 LotteryID)
  • Choice Number: the order ranking of each entry in the application (1, 2, and 3 per application)
  • Preferred Entry Date: the preferred entry date specified for each application entry
  • Preferred Zone: the preferred zone specified for each application entry
  • Maximum Requested Group Size: the requested group each application entry
  • Choice Awarded?: whether or not the individual entry of the applicaiton was awarded
  • Application Result: whether or not any one of the three entries in the application
So, for the same example above, the data would look like this:

Lottery Year LotteryID Choice ID Choice Number Preferred Entry Date Preferred Zone Maximum Requested Group Size Choice Awarded? Application Result
2024 2024_44 2024_44_1 1 5/15/2025 Colchuck Zone 4 Yes Awarded
2024 2024_44 2024_44_2 2 8/7/2025 Core Enchantment Zone 7 No Awarded
2024 2024_44 2024_44_3 3 7/1/2025 Eightmile Zone 3 No Awarded

In this example, the first application entry was awarded, which makes the overal application successful. So, even though the second and third entries were not awarded, they are still part of a successful application.

Analysis

Where Are the Enchantments?

This interactive map zooms in on the Alpine Lakes Wilderness and highlights the 5 permit zones within the Enchantments area: Colchuck, Core, Eightmile/Caroline, Snow, and Stuart. These zones represent the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd preferences that you can specify on your application. Click on the arrows at the top to see where this area is with reference to Seattle and Western Washington.

What are the Overall Chances of Winning?

This dashboard highlights the raw probailities of having a specific application entry and overall application approved. It also helps us understand the odds of success over the time range that the Enchantments are open (5/15 to 10/31). Click the fullscreen icon to view the entire dashboard.


We can see from our dashboard that the success rate for winning the lottery is 6.44%, while the award rate is 2.16%. The success rate represents the rate of applications that were successful in grating access to one of the entries in the application. The award rate represents the probability of success for an individual entry in an application.

Exploring Odds Over Time

Here we can begin to understand how to increase our odds of success. We can see that entries with preferred entry dates in October have the highest success at 11.28%, while August provides the lowest. Click the full screen icon in the bottom right to expand the dashboard.


We also learn that entry dates on Sunday are most likely to be approved, with a 3.5% award rate. We want to avoid applying for Fridays though, as we have the lowest chance of success on those days.

What Zones Should we Target?

This visual helps us understand the individual award rates for different zones. The bar chart provides the probabilities, while the map gives reference to where each of these zones is.


We see that the Eightmile/Caroline zone provides the best odds of success at almost 12%; a margin of almost 6% over the next best Stuart zone. The Colchuck and Core Enchantment zones don't look as promising, but there are other ways we can increase our chances for those zones.

What Group Sizes Work Best?

In this visual the colored bars (zones) are clustered by group size from 1 to 8. The darker opaque bars represent the odds of success for each group size, regardless of the zone. We can see a clear pattern: smaller group sizes have a higher chance of success. For some zones, like the Eightmile/Caroline zone, solo applicants have a 16.28% chance of winning. Click the fullscreen icon to view the entire dashboard.


So, if you want to maximize our chances of going to the more competitive zones like Core Enchantment or Colchuck, you would want to apply solo. If you don't want to go solo, then there are other ways we can guarantee success.

Maximize Your Odds

This dashboard gives you everything you need to maximize your chances of success for different combinations of zones, time of year, and group sizes. The table only shows instances where application entries had a over a 50% award rate. You can click on the map to specify what zone you'd prefer, and the table will filter out for different scenarios for each zone. The Zone Size Pie chart and time charts work the same way. Click the fullscreen icon to view the entire dashboard.


For example, let's say I only want to visit the Core Enchantment zone. If I click on the Core Enchantment zone on the map, then I wil see that there are two scenarios where I have over a 50% chance of my application entry being awarded: Weekday: Wednesday or Saturday, Group Size: 1, Month: May. You can use this tool to fine tune your preferences and give yourself the best chance at success for exactly what you want.

Adding Weather Data

Now that we have a good understanding of how to maximize our odds, let's try to target some dates where the weather is likely to be good. We found daily weather data for the Enchantments dating back to 1/1/1981 that included the precipitation in inches and the low, average, and high temperature of each day. We averaged out this data for each day over the 43 years and merged it with our 2024 lottery results to compare our chances against the historical weather for each day. Below is the aggregate weather data positioned above the odds of an awarded application entry.


Unsurprisingly, the chances of winning the lottery are positively correlated with the historical average precipitation and negatively correlated with the historical average temperature. This is also directly correlated with the number of applications (we can cross-reference the charts here with the applications over time seen in the chart above); more people want to go to the Enchantments on nicer days, which means that odds of wining the lottery for nicer days is significantly lower.

Final Recommendation

Let's say we want to target "nice" days to go to the Enchantments: days where the historical aggregate average is over 52 degrees Farenheit and the average precipitation is below 0.03 inches. Below, we've filtered our above chart for only those days, and we've added the Success Rate by Group Size chart. Interacting with this visual can help us pick nice days where the overall award percentage is highest, like July 2nd and September 2nd, and see how we can maximize our chances for success. Click the fullscreen icon to view the entire dashboard.


If we select July 2nd, which has a 12% chance of being awarded based on historical data, the chart on the right will tell us that we should apply for the Eightmile or Stuart zones with group sizes of 4 and 5, respectively. These zone/group combinations give us a 50% chance of our application entry being awarded. Each of the days on the left-side chart can be selected to view which zone/group combos yield the best odds for that particular day.
