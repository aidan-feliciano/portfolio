The Enchantments Lottery — March 2025

Background

The Enchantments is a region in Washington State’s Cascade Range with hundreds of alpine lakes and ponds surrounded by dramatic granite peaks. It is one of the most popular destinations for hikers, campers, rock climbers, and anyone who enjoys the wilderness. To address heavy demand and reduce overcrowding, land managers introduced an overnight permit lottery system.

How the Lottery Works

The lottery opens on February 15 and closes on March 1 each year. Each applicant may submit one application with three ranked entries (zone, date, and group size). The ranking is determined by the order in the application. For example:

Example lottery application with three ranked entries
Zone Entry Date Group Size
Colchuck 5/15/2024 4
Core Enchantments 8/7/2024 7
Eightmile 7/1/2024 3

An applicant can only apply once, which reduces the odds of any single application winning. However, success is not purely random—some choices dramatically change your chances. This project highlights how to improve your odds and recommends approaches to maximize them.

Data Overview

Two datasets power this analysis: (1) 2024 Enchantments lottery statistics and (2) historical daily weather for the area. Each observation in the lottery data represents an application entry (as in the example above) and includes:

  • Lottery Year: the year in which the application was submitted (2024 for all data shown here)
  • LotteryID: the identifier for a single application
  • Choice ID: the identifier for an individual entry within that application (3 per LotteryID)
  • Choice Number: the rank of each entry (1, 2, or 3)
  • Preferred Entry Date: the target entry date for the permit
  • Preferred Zone: the zone requested for that entry
  • Maximum Requested Group Size: the requested group size for each entry
  • Choice Awarded?: whether the individual entry was awarded
  • Application Result: whether any one of the three entries was awarded

The same example represented as rows in the dataset:

Example application represented as three dataset rows
Lottery Year LotteryID Choice ID Choice Number Preferred Entry Date Preferred Zone Maximum Requested Group Size Choice Awarded? Application Result

In this example, Entry #1 was awarded, so the overall application is successful—even though Entries #2 and #3 were not.

Analysis

Where Are the Enchantments?

This interactive map zooms in on the Alpine Lakes Wilderness and highlights five permit zones within the Enchantments area: Colchuck, Core Enchantments, Eightmile/Caroline, Snow, and Stuart. These zones correspond to the ranked preferences you can specify on your application. Use the story navigation to orient the area relative to Seattle and Western Washington.

What Are the Overall Chances of Winning?

This dashboard highlights the raw probabilities of having a specific application entry awarded and of the overall application succeeding. It also shows how odds vary across the operating season (5/15–10/31). Click the fullscreen icon to view the entire dashboard.

From this view, the success rate (any entry awarded) is 6.44%, while the award rate (individual entry awarded) is 2.16%.

Exploring Odds Over Time

Here we begin to see how timing matters. Entries with preferred dates in October have the highest success at 11.28%, while August has the lowest. Click the fullscreen icon in the bottom-right to expand.

Sundays show the highest award rate at 3.5%, while Fridays are the most competitive.

What Zones Should We Target?

This view shows individual award rates by zone alongside a reference map.

Eightmile/Caroline provides the best odds at ~12%, roughly six percentage points above Stuart. Colchuck and Core Enchantments are more competitive, but we can still improve our chances there through timing and group size.

What Group Sizes Work Best?

Colored bars (zones) are clustered by group size (1–8). Darker, opaque bars show the overall odds by group size across zones. The pattern is clear: smaller groups have higher success. In Eightmile/Caroline, solo applicants reach 16.28%. Click the fullscreen icon to view the entire dashboard.

If you aim for Core Enchantments or Colchuck, applying solo meaningfully improves your chances. If you prefer not to go solo, the next section shows ways to increase odds through timing and zone selection.

Maximize Your Odds

This dashboard helps you maximize success for combinations of zones, time of year, and group sizes. The table lists only scenarios where entries exceeded a 50% award rate. Click the map to select a zone; charts and the table filter accordingly. The group-size pie and time charts work similarly. Click the fullscreen icon to view the entire dashboard.

Example: If you only want Core Enchantments, selecting that zone shows two scenarios with >50% entry award probability: Day: Wednesday or Saturday, Group Size: 1, Month: May. Use the controls to fine-tune preferences for your exact goal.

Adding Weather Data

To target comfortable conditions, we incorporated daily historical averages (1981–present): precipitation (inches) and low/average/high temperatures (°F). We averaged each calendar day across years and compared those values to 2024 lottery outcomes to explore relationships. These are long-term averages, not forecasts; actual conditions vary.

Unsurprisingly, award odds move with conditions: applications are most competitive (lower odds) on historically warmer, drier days when more people apply.

Final Recommendation

Suppose we define “nice” days as those with avg temp ≥ 52°F and avg precip ≤ 0.03 in. The view below filters to those days and pairs them with success-by-group-size. Interact to find high-odds windows (e.g., July 2 and September 2) and see which zone/group combinations give you the best shot. Click the fullscreen icon to view the entire dashboard.

For example, selecting July 2 (≈12% award rate historically) suggests Eightmile (group size 4) or Stuart (group size 5) to reach ~50% entry award odds for that date.

Conclusion & Next Steps

Your odds improve most by timing (late season, mid‑week), zone selection (Eightmile/Caroline > Stuart > Colchuck/Core), and group size (smaller is better). Weather preferences can be layered on top, but expect trade‑offs: the “nicer” the day historically, the more competitive the lottery.

  • Pick 2–3 acceptable zones; include at least one less competitive option.
  • Favor October or shoulder weeks; avoid peak‑demand Fridays.
  • Go solo or reduce group size when targeting Core Enchantments/Colchuck.
  • Use the “Maximize Your Odds” view to identify >50% scenarios for your exact constraints.
  • Remember: historical weather is not a forecast—always plan for variance.
