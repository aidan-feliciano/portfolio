Seattle Building Permits Analysis - July 2024

Problem

How is construction activity distributed across Seattle? We aimed to understand where building activity is concentrated, what types of projects are underway, and how they're being executed across the city by using permit data to uncover trends and draw actionable insights for real estate stakeholders and potential homebuyers.

Dataset Overview

We used 2023 building permit data from the City of Seattle Open Data Portal. The dataset contains 5,596 records, each representing a unique building permit. There were 25 columns describing each permit, such as project cost, permit class, housing units, ZIP code, and more.

Goal / Key Metrics

Our objective was to identify insights that could help real estate investors or homebuyers target neighborhoods to purchase or invest in. Key metrics included:

  • Identifying high-growth ZIP codes
  • Comparing commercial vs. residential permit activity
  • Estimating project costs and approval timelines

Actions

  • Data cleansing and preprocessing: Addressing missing values, data spillage, and correcting datatypes.
  • Exploratory data analysis: Visualizing permits by class and ZIP code, plotting distributions for approval times and permit costs.

Findings

High-growth ZIP codes

Seattle ZIP codes with the most permits in 2023:

  • 98115: Roosevelt, Ravenna, Wedgwood
  • 98103: Greenlake, Wallingford, Greenwood
  • 98117: Sunset Hill, Ballard
  • 98118: Rainier Valley, Seward Park, Rainier Beach

Permit approval times

50% of permits were approved within 50 days. The average approval time was 79 days.

Most expensive ZIP codes

  • 98121: Belltown
  • 98109: Queen Anne
  • 98108: South Seattle, Beacon Hill
  • 98101: Downtown Seattle

Key Insights

Investors and buyers looking for areas of high development might consider ZIPs 98115, 98103, 98117, and 98118. Developers may use project cost data to allocate finances strategically. Those seeking quieter neighborhoods should target ZIPs with fewer permits.

Next Steps

  • Integrate with property values or rental rates
  • Analyze seasonality trends over time
  • Use clustering/PCA for dimensionality reduction

Tools

  • Python (pandas, matplotlib, seaborn)
  • SQL for querying and cleanup
