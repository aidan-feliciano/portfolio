Seattle Building Permits Analysis

Problem

How is construction activity distributed across Seattle? We aimed to understand where, what, and how building projects are happening across the city by using permit data to uncover trends and draw actionable insights for real estate stakeholders and potential homebuyers.

Dataset Overview

We used 2023 building permit data from the City of Seattle Open Data Portal. The dataset contains 5,596 records, each representing a unique building permit. There were 25 columns representing attributes for each permit, such as project cost, permit class, housing units, zip code, and more.

Goal / Key Metrics

Our objective was to identify insights that could help real estate investors or homebuyers target neighborhoods in which to purchase or invest. Key metrics included:

  • Identifying high-growth zip codes
  • Comparing commercial vs. residential permit activity
  • Estimating project costs and approval timelines

Actions

We completed several steps during our analysis:

  • Data cleansing and preprocessing: Addressing missing values, data spillage, and correcting datatypes.
  • Exploratory data analysis: Visualizing permits by class and zipcode, plotting distributions for permit approval time and permit costs.

Findings

High-growth zip codes:

The zip codes in Seattle and their corresponding neighborhoods with the highest number of permits in 2023 were:

  • 98115: Roosevelt, Ravenna, Wedgewood
  • 98103: Greenlake, Wallingford, Greenwood
  • 98117: Sunset Hill, Ballard
  • 98118: Rainier Valley, Seward Park, Rainier Beach



Permit approval times:

50% of permits were approved within 50 days, while the average approval time was 79 days.


The zip codes with the most expensive estimated project costs were:

  • 98121: Belltown
  • 98109: Queen Anne
  • 98108: South Seattle, Beacon Hill
  • 98101: Downtown Seattle

Key Insights

Real estate investors or potential home buyers who want to purchase properties in areas with a lot of active or planned development (i.e., areas that may boom in the next few years and therefore bring more traffic or drive up real estate prices) may want to consider building in the ZIP codes with high numbers of permits: 98115, 98103, 98117, and 98118. Potential home buyers looking for quiet neighborhoods that will not experience significant change or development may want to focus on the ZIP codes with fewer permits.

Additionally, by knowing the average project costs for each Zip Code, developers can better allocate their finances. They also may be able to execute more financially feasible projects by selecting areas with lower average project costs.
